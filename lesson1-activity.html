Instructions:

  • • Launch the virtual PC building simulator on your assigned computer or device.
  • • Read the Checklist and criteria down Below
  • • Familiarize yourself with the virtual interface: know how to zoom, rotate the case, and select or remove components.

2. Step-by-Step Assembly Process (15 minutes)

A. Install the Motherboard (3 minutes)
  • • Select the compatible motherboard for the system.
  • • Place the motherboard into the case, aligning it with the standoffs.
  • • Secure it using screws, ensuring it is firmly attached.
B. Attach the CPU (2 minutes)
  • • Open the CPU socket lever.
  • • Align the CPU properly with the socket markers.
  • • Secure the CPU with the locking mechanism.
C. Insert RAM Modules (2 minutes)
  • • Select the correct RAM module type.
  • • Align the module with the motherboard slot.
  • • Press down firmly until the RAM clicks into place.
D. Install Power Supply and Connect Cables (3 minutes)
  • • Mount the power supply into its compartment.
  • • Connect the 24-pin motherboard power cable.
  • • Attach the CPU power connector and other necessary power cables.
E. Attach Storage Devices (HDD/SSD) (2 minutes)
  • • Secure the storage devices in their designated slots.
  • • Connect power and data cables to the motherboard.
F. Install Cooling System (2 minutes)
  • • Mount the cooling system (air or liquid cooling) as required.
  • • Secure cooling fans and connect them to the appropriate headers.
G. Connect Peripheral Cables (1 minute)
  • • Attach USB, HDMI, and other necessary cables to the motherboard and case ports.

3. Functional Testing & Troubleshooting (3 minutes)

  • • Power on the system and check for POST (Power-On Self-Test) success.
  • • Identify any errors, such as missing components or incorrect connections.
  • • Troubleshoot issues as needed.

4. Organization & Documentation (2 minutes)

  • • Organize cables neatly for proper airflow and aesthetics.
  • • Document the assembly steps taken or provide an assembly report.

What to Consider?

Here are five key things to remember when assembling computer hardware:

  1. 1. Compatibility is Key – Ensure all components (CPU, motherboard, RAM, GPU, PSU) are compatible to avoid issues.
  2. 2. Proper Cooling & Airflow – Prevent overheating by using appropriate cooling solutions and ensuring good airflow inside the case.
  3. 3. Secure & Organized Cable Management – Properly connect and route cables to avoid clutter, improve airflow, and make troubleshooting easier.
  4. 4. Power Supply Consideration – Choose a PSU with sufficient wattage and reliable efficiency (80 Plus Bronze or higher) to support all components.
  5. 5. Double-Check Connections Before Powering On – Verify that all cables, RAM, storage devices, and cooling fans are securely connected to avoid boot failures.
